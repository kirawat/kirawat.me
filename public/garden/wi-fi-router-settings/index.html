Dual-band

  • 2.4 GHz has better range but can be more crowded.
  • 5 GHz offers faster speeds but has shorter range.

For dual-band routers, consider using 5 GHz for high-bandwidth activities (like gaming or streaming) and 2.4 GHz for general usage.

Channel

If experiencing slow speeds, try changing the Wi-Fi channel to avoid interference from other networks.

Frequency Bandwidth

Frequency Bandwidth determines the width of the frequency range the router uses to transmit data over Wi-Fi. It’s like a highway for data, a wider bandwidth means more data can travel simultaneously, leading to faster speeds.

  • 20 MHz: The most common and compatible option, offering stable connections but lower speeds.

  • 40 MHz: Doubles the bandwidth, potentially increasing speeds but might be less stable in crowded areas.

  • 80 MHz: Available on some newer routers, provides even greater speeds but requires compatible devices and less interference.

Guard Interval (GI)

Guard interval (GI) prevent interference between consecutive data transmissions. It acts like a buffer zone between symbols, protecting against unwanted signal overlap and ensuring accurate data reception. This is especially beneficial in challenging environments with obstacles or dense Wi-Fi networks.

In wireless data transmission, the signal can take multiple paths due to obstacles or reflections. This can lead to echoes and overlapping symbols, impacting data integrity. The guard interval is like a gap inserted between each symbol. It gives echoes time to fade before the next symbol arrives, ensuring each symbol is clearly distinct and accurately received.

Reduced interference from echoes and signal reflections leading to fewer errors and makes the connection more stable and more resistant to multipath propagation which common in complex environments.

Longer guard intervals offer better reliability and stronger echo attenuation which helps extend the reach of the Wi-Fi signal but has lower throughput. It also reduces the overall data transfer rate because less time is used for actual data transmission. This might be noticeable for bandwidth-intensive activities like streaming or gaming.

Shorter guard intervals has higher throughput which maximizes data transmission efficiency, leading to faster Wi-Fi speeds. This is ideal for situations where raw speed is prioritize over absolute reliability. However, it’s more susceptible to interference and signal reflections, potentially leading to connection instability and data errors. This can be problematic in environments with many obstacles or competing Wi-Fi networks. The signal might not even reach as far due to weaker echo attenuation.

Choose Long Guard Interval in crowed or complex environments with lots of obstacles or interference. For open spaces with clean signals, Short Guard Interval might be better.

Prioritize Long Guard Interval for reliable connections even at lower speeds. If raw speed is paramount, Short Guard Interval might be better despite potential instability.
